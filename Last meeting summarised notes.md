# **Composition Studio - Logical Summary & Direction**

## **1. The Core Problem We Are Trying To Solve**

### **Traditional Composition Education**

Most composition courses begin with:

- Harmony
- Counterpoint
- Form
- Orchestration

Students learn:

- Rules
- Techniques
- Structures

But often still struggle to answer:

- How do I actually write music?
- How do I start a piece?
- Why choose one musical idea over another?

### **Alex’s Criticism**

The problem is that:

```text
Technique often comes before meaning.
```

Students learn how to manipulate music before understanding where musical ideas come from.

---

## **2. Alex’s Core Philosophy**

### **Music Begins Before Notes**

Composition does not begin with:

- Chords
- Harmony
- Counterpoint
- Form

It begins with:

- Experience
- Character
- Image
- Emotion
- Dramatic situation
- Human expression

### **The Fundamental Question**

Not:

```text
What is a motive?
```

But:

```text
Why this motive?
```

Why does one idea feel convincing while another feels empty?

### **The Educational Goal**

Help students:

- Recognise inner experiences
- Translate them into musical gestures
- Turn gestures into motives
- Develop motives into music

## **4. What Students Commonly Do Wrong**

### **Common Motive Problems**

Students often create motives that are:

- Constantly active
- Constantly loud
- Constantly important

Missing:

- Contrast
- Space
- Breathing

### **Students Ignore Rests**

Important principle:

```text
Silence is part of the music.
```

Rests can be:

- Expressive
- Structural
- Dramatic

### **Students Ignore Expressive Parameters**

Students focus on:

- Pitch
- Rhythm

But neglect:

- Dynamics
- Articulation
- Tempo
- Character
- Phrasing

---

## **5. Educational Philosophy**

### **Compose First**

The course should teach:

```text
Composition through composing.
```

Not:

```text
Theory first.
Composition later.
```

### **Necessary Information Principle**

Students should learn:

- Only the harmony they currently need
- Only the counterpoint they currently need
- Only the form they currently need

To solve the current compositional problem.

Advanced theory comes later.

---

## **6. Project-Based Learning**

### **Traditional Model**

```text
Lesson
↓
Exercise
↓
Quiz
```

### **Proposed Model**

```text
Project
↓
Learn concepts as needed
↓
Create music
```

### **Example**

Project:

Write a Four-Bar Melody

Student receives:

- Image
- Character
- Scene
- Prompt

Then learns:

- Gesture
- Motive
- Phrase
- Development

While creating actual music.

---

## **7. Guided Creativity**

### **The Blank Page Problem**

Many students freeze when told:

```text
Write a melody.
```

### **Solution**

Provide:

- Images
- Character descriptions
- Rhythmic suggestions
- Alternative note choices
- Example solutions

Students receive:

- Freedom
- Support
- Confidence

### **Multiple Correct Answers**

Important principle:

```text
Art does not have one correct solution.
```

Ten students should create ten different pieces.

---

## **8. Repetition & Mastery**

### **Problem**

Most courses teach a skill once and move on.

### **Proposed Solution**

Repeated Projects

Project 1:

- Motive
- Melody

Project 2:

- Same skills
- Different context

Project 3:

- Same skills
- One new concept

Project 4:

- Same skills
- Different emotional goal

Students repeatedly use skills until they become natural.
